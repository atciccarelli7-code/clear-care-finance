# CAF Survival and Revenue Gates

Effective date: 2026-07-12

## Bear case

Community Acquired Finance will not become durable by adding more pages faster than it proves discovery, trust, return behavior, and willingness to pay. The last repository-backed analytics snapshot recorded 181 visitors and 459 pageviews. At that scale, display advertising is not a business model.

Illustrative gross monthly display revenue before any deductions, invalid traffic adjustments, unfilled inventory, restricted inventory, or ad-free routes:

| Total monthly pageviews | $5 page RPM scenario | $10 page RPM scenario | $20 page RPM scenario |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 459 | $2.30 | $4.59 | $9.18 |
| 1,000 | $5 | $10 | $20 |
| 10,000 | $50 | $100 | $200 |
| 25,000 | $125 | $250 | $500 |
| 100,000 | $500 | $1,000 | $2,000 |

These are scenarios, not CAF performance forecasts. CAF's actual ad-eligible pageviews are lower than total pageviews because product, Receipt, homepage, legal, and other protected routes remain ad-free.

## Failure modes

1. **Supply outruns demand.** The sitemap contains 142 canonical URLs, including 63 articles and 34 focused tool routes, without current Search Console evidence showing which non-brand queries produce impressions or clicks.
2. **Trust remains founder-limited.** RN bedside experience is relevant to healthcare confusion, but it is not a substitute for credentialed financial, tax, legal, insurance, Medicare, or benefits-administration review.
3. **Owned retention is unproven.** Newsletter delivery, durable non-owner contact capture, the first issue, unsubscribe operations, and recurring production cadence require live evidence.
4. **Display ads are arithmetically immaterial.** Increasing ad density before traffic grows can reduce trust and product completion for single-digit or low-double-digit monthly revenue.
5. **No buyer means no business.** Consumer utility can be real while revenue remains zero. A low-risk organization pilot must prove that a defined buyer will pay for a defined education problem without creating unsupported enterprise claims.
6. **Prerendering can conceal a dead application.** A page can show complete HTML while a broken production chunk graph prevents React from hydrating. Production verification must include a state-changing interaction, not only screenshots and HTTP status checks.

## Ninety-day survival gates

Targets below are operating hypotheses. They are not current results.

### Distribution gate

- Obtain verified Search Console exports for 7 days, 28 days, 3 months, and full available history.
- Track non-brand impressions, clicks, indexed canonicals, query-to-page fit, and pages ranking in positions 8–30.
- Improve existing pages with demonstrated impressions before authorizing another broad content batch.
- Stop creating new overlapping articles if no existing cluster gains non-brand impressions after two complete crawl-and-measure cycles.

### Trust gate

- Every article visibly identifies the author, scope of credentials, review status, source method, review date, and correction route.
- No page claims independent professional review unless a named qualified reviewer actually completed it.
- Prioritize external professional review for the ten pages with the highest combination of traffic and potential harm.

### Owned-audience gate

- Verify a production signup using a non-owner address with contact persistence, accurate UI state, welcome delivery, unsubscribe handling, sender authentication, and clean runtime logs.
- Publish the first issue before promising an ongoing archive or increasing signup prominence.
- Report signup conversion as counts until the sample is large enough for a rate to be meaningful.

### Product gate

- Measure consented acquisition-to-tool, Concierge completion, tool completion, Receipt action, next-journey, and safe return events.
- Treat a product route as successful only when visitors complete or act—not because it exists or prerenders.
- Remove or consolidate product entry points that create choice without measurable use after sufficient traffic.

### Revenue gate

- Do not optimize display-ad density until CAF records at least 25,000 monthly ad-eligible content pageviews for three consecutive months and can show that ads do not materially reduce page speed, completion, or trust signals.
- Run ten qualified organization discovery conversations before representing the pilot offer as validated.
- Seek three defined pilot evaluations and one written pilot commitment before building employer accounts, integrations, dashboards, or administration features.
- Keep pricing as a hypothesis until a buyer accepts scope and price.

## Pilot pricing hypotheses—not validated prices

- Design-partner pilot: no fee or cost recovery only, in exchange for structured access and feedback.
- Fixed educational pilot: $1,500–$5,000 for one cohort, one decision moment, and four to six weeks.
- Future annual education license: define only after a pilot proves participation, usefulness, support load, privacy boundaries, and buyer willingness to renew.

No range should appear as a quote, market benchmark, or validated willingness-to-pay claim.

## Stop rules

- If no non-brand discovery improves after two evidence-led optimization cycles, stop adding pages and reconsider the market/focus.
- If production email capture cannot be verified, remove or reduce signup promises rather than collecting failed intent.
- If ten qualified organization conversations produce no clear buyer, urgent problem, or pilot interest, do not build enterprise infrastructure.
- If display ads reduce product completion, readability, or trust, remove them from the affected surface regardless of short-term revenue.

## Weekly operating review

Record only evidence: Search Console query/page exports, analytics counts, newsletter delivery evidence, product completion, organization conversations, pilot-stage movement, runtime errors, and revenue. Documentation volume, route count, word count, and feature count are not survival metrics.
