# Controlled buyer-review brief

## Purpose

The Blood Thinner Care Readiness demonstration includes a fixed-choice institutional brief that helps a hospital stakeholder determine the next safe conversation without submitting patient information, free text, internal records, or medication instructions.

## Inputs

- Organization type.
- Stakeholder role.
- First setting.
- Primary decision problem.
- Current review stage.
- Internal owner coverage.
- Explicit no-PHI boundary confirmation.

Selections remain in the browser tab. They are not placed in the URL or emitted as answer-level analytics.

## Result states

### Discovery only

Used when an accountable sponsor plus nursing and pharmacy owners are not yet identified. The result permits problem discovery but states that pilot planning is premature.

### Controlled review ready

Used when nursing and pharmacy ownership exists. The next step is a cross-functional workflow and governance review involving patient education, quality, and an operational sponsor.

### Design-partner pathway can be evaluated

Used only when the organization reports a cross-functional sponsor/review team and is evaluating a design partnership or procurement path. This result still does not indicate pilot readiness, hospital approval, or patient-use authorization.

## Stop conditions

- No accountable clinical or operational owner.
- Request to distribute medication instructions before exact-version review and hospital approval.
- Need to send PHI or case narratives through the public site.
- Missing pharmacy, after-hours, monitoring, correction, or recall ownership.
- Expectation of guaranteed clinical or economic outcomes.

## Public/private boundary

The public brief covers problem framing, owners, workflow, governance, and next steps. It does not expose medication-specific instructions, private evidence mapping, reviewer identities, hospital-localized language, editable masters, or commercial terms.
