# Localization and implementation checklist

## Clinical and operational localization

- [ ] Approve eligible populations, exclusions, care settings, and clinical owners.
- [ ] Bind each exact formulary product, strength, schedule, and labeling source.
- [ ] Approve bleeding, fall/head-injury, procedure, missed-dose escalation, and emergency language.
- [ ] Supply daytime, after-hours, pharmacy, monitoring, laboratory, and follow-up routes.
- [ ] Define authorized staff roles, documentation location, escalation chain, and safe backup standards.
- [ ] Reconcile existing discharge documents so the module does not introduce a conflicting plan.
- [ ] Configure interpreter, translation, large-print, digital accessibility, caregiver, and disability supports.
- [ ] Approve print stock, distribution points, version display, replacement, retirement, and recall workflow.

## Validation before limited pilot

- [ ] Independent clinical and pharmacy review complete; critical claims dual-reviewed.
- [ ] Health-literacy review and PEMAT assessment complete.
- [ ] Keyboard, screen-reader, zoom/reflow, contrast, focus, and print checks complete.
- [ ] Patient/caregiver testing includes medicine matching, missed-dose, emergency, procedure, refill, and barrier tasks.
- [ ] Staff simulation includes nights/weekends, unavailable medicine, cost/authorization, interpreter, caregiver, and conflicting-instruction scenarios.
- [ ] Measurement, privacy, security, support, correction, incident, and recall plans approved.
- [ ] Named release authority signs the exact manifest, language, organization, version, and effective period.

## Go-live boundary

Do not copy synthetic review-state storage or website analytics into a clinical environment. The institutional deployment requires an approved system of record, role controls, auditability, retention rules, and security review.
