# Diagnosis, Explained — Source-to-Claim Governance Matrix

**Created:** July 22, 2026  
**Applies to:** Heart Failure, Explained and COPD, Explained  
**Release status:** Internal source-control record; both patient guides remain clinical-review previews, noindex, and ad-free.

## Purpose

This matrix prevents a reputable but outdated or overly general patient page from silently becoming the authority for a time-sensitive clinical statement. Every high-risk claim category must have a named primary source, a defined secondary use, an explicit patient-education boundary, and a refresh trigger.

## Source tiers

| Tier | Appropriate use | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 — professional or regulatory authority** | Definitions, diagnostic standards, treatment frameworks, medication eligibility, oxygen or rehabilitation recommendations, and other clinically consequential claims. | Professional society guidelines and consensus documents; GOLD; FDA regulatory material. |
| **Tier 2 — federal or specialty patient education** | Plain-language wording, practical self-management, common questions, warning-sign framing, and reinforcement of Tier 1 claims. | AHA patient resources, NHLBI, CDC, ATS patient resources, American Lung Association. |
| **Tier 3 — external coverage benchmark** | Completeness comparison, conventional terminology, appointment-preparation topics, and usability benchmarking. Not the sole authority for medication, oxygen, diagnostic, or emergency claims. | Mayo Clinic, Cleveland Clinic, MedlinePlus, WebMD. |

## Heart failure matrix

| Claim domain | Primary authority | Secondary support | Boundary used in the guide | Refresh trigger |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Definition and EF phenotype | 2026 AHA/ACC/ESC/WHF Second Universal Definition | AHA and Mayo patient education | Explains a clinician-given diagnosis; does not diagnose or interpret one EF result. | New universal definition or major society classification update. |
| A-to-D stage and NYHA functional class | 2026 universal definition; 2022 AHA/ACC/HFSA guideline | AHA Classes and Stages patient page | Type, stage, and functional class are separate systems; the care team assigns them. | New guideline or AHA patient classification revision. |
| HFrEF foundational therapy | 2022 AHA/ACC/HFSA guideline; 2024 ACC HFrEF decision pathway | AHA medication education | Describes medication groups and jobs; does not select drugs, sequence therapy, or set doses. | New HFrEF pathway, guideline, major safety change, or label change. |
| HFpEF treatment framing | 2023 ACC HFpEF decision pathway; 2022 guideline | AHA patient education | Describes goals and comorbidity management; does not infer eligibility or treatment from a general guide. | New HFpEF pathway, guideline, or major trial incorporated into guidance. |
| Medication monitoring and safety boundaries | Professional guidelines and current drug-specific regulatory information when needed | AHA medication education | Monitoring is described as care-team work; no dose-change instructions. | Guideline, boxed warning, contraindication, or major label revision. |
| Devices and advanced therapy | 2022 guideline and relevant professional pathways | Mayo as coverage benchmark | Explains purpose and selected eligibility; does not determine candidacy. | Device guideline or regulatory change. |
| Home monitoring and escalation | AHA warning-sign and action-plan education | Mayo and NHLBI as corroboration | Individualized written thresholds replace generic examples; emergency services control emergency response. | AHA warning-sign revision or clinician-review correction. |
| Lifestyle and living with heart failure | AHA and current professional guidance | NHLBI as supplemental general support | NHLBI's 2022 treatment page is not used as authority for current medication or HFpEF claims. | Source update or new professional lifestyle recommendation. |

## COPD matrix

| Claim domain | Primary authority | Secondary support | Boundary used in the guide | Refresh trigger |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Definition, diagnosis, and spirometry | GOLD 2026 | NHLBI diagnosis; CDC | Explains a confirmed diagnosis; does not interpret individual spirometry. Imaging does not replace spirometry. | Annual GOLD update or spirometry standard revision. |
| Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency testing | COPD Foundation/Alpha-1 Foundation clinical practice guideline; GOLD | NHLBI and ATS patient education | Prompts a one-time testing question; does not interpret genotype or treatment eligibility. | GOLD or AATD guideline update. |
| Rescue and maintenance bronchodilators | GOLD 2026 | NHLBI treatment | Separates purpose and device roles; no dose, schedule, substitution, or maximum-use instructions. | Annual GOLD update, drug label change, or device safety change. |
| Inhaled corticosteroid-containing therapy | GOLD 2026 | NHLBI treatment | Explains selected use and pneumonia/thrush considerations; does not start or remove the steroid component. | Annual GOLD update or major safety/eosinophil-selection change. |
| Flare-up medicines | GOLD 2026 | American Lung Association action-plan material | No self-starting leftover steroids or antibiotics without a current explicit written plan. | Annual GOLD update, antimicrobial guidance change, or reviewer correction. |
| Less-common specialist medicines | GOLD 2026 and drug-specific FDA information | Mayo as a terminology/coverage benchmark | Names narrow options without presenting a treatment checklist; specialist eligibility and monitoring control. | GOLD update, new approval, label expansion/restriction, or safety communication. |
| Pulmonary rehabilitation | ATS 2023 pulmonary-rehabilitation guideline; GOLD | NHLBI pulmonary rehabilitation | Explains benefits and access questions; the clinician determines timing and program suitability. | ATS or GOLD rehabilitation update. |
| Home oxygen | ATS home-oxygen guideline; GOLD | NHLBI and CDC | Oxygen treats measured hypoxemia; prescription controls flow and timing; fire safety is explicit. | ATS/GOLD update, equipment safety alert, or reviewer correction. |
| Action plan and escalation | GOLD 2026 | American Lung Association management tools | Uses baseline/prompt-contact/emergency organization without prescribing flare-up treatment. | Annual GOLD update, ALA action-plan revision, or reviewer correction. |

## Required evidence before publication

Before either guide can move from `clinical-review` to a publishable state:

1. A qualified independent reviewer must verify every Tier 1 claim domain listed above.
2. Reviewer name, credentials, review date, scope, comments, and resolved corrections must be recorded.
3. Every external source link must be rechecked.
4. Medication, oxygen, and emergency language must receive claim-level sign-off.
5. The route must remain noindex and ad-free until the clinical-review record is complete.

## Maintenance rule

A source appearing in the public source list does not automatically authorize every statement in the guide. The matrix controls which source tier supports which claim. When sources conflict, the current professional or regulatory authority controls, and the discrepancy must be documented rather than silently reconciled.
