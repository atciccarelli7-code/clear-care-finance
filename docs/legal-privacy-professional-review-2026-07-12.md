# Legal, Privacy, Advertising, and Professional Review Register

**Assessment date:** July 12, 2026

## Purpose

This register identifies legal, regulatory, privacy, advertising, and data-handling issues that cannot be eliminated through interface design, disclaimers, or automated tests alone.

Community Acquired Finance provides general educational information and decision preparation. It is not a law firm, tax practice, investment adviser, insurance agency, medical practice, claims administrator, benefits administrator, or government agency.

No technical control or disclaimer can guarantee that the website will never face a legal claim, regulatory inquiry, policy change, security incident, or factual error.

## Policies suitable for qualified attorney review

A qualified attorney should review, at minimum:

1. Terms of Use and limitation-of-liability language.
2. Privacy Policy accuracy and state-law applicability.
3. Disclosures and educational-advice boundaries.
4. Advertising Policy and route-level ad exclusions.
5. Editorial and Corrections policies.
6. Accessibility statement and contact process.
7. Governing-law, dispute, and jurisdiction provisions.
8. Contact-form warnings and data-retention statements.
9. Any future affiliate, referral, sponsorship, lead-generation, or paid-placement agreement.
10. Any future account, authentication, email-reminder, upload, or cross-device synchronization system.

Do not state or imply that these documents have received legal review until that review actually occurs.

## State-specific content requiring review or heightened sourcing

The following topics vary materially by state and should remain routing and verification content unless separately researched and reviewed:

- Medicaid financial eligibility;
- Medicaid long-term-care functional eligibility;
- resource and transfer rules;
- spousal impoverishment protections;
- estate recovery;
- home- and community-based waiver availability and waiting lists;
- medically needy pathways;
- state surprise-billing protections;
- medical-debt collection protections;
- insurance appeal rights;
- Medigap guaranteed-issue and underwriting rights outside federal minimum rules;
- state tax treatment of retirement contributions and withdrawals;
- employment, wage, overtime, leave, and benefit-continuation rules.

The website should route visitors to current official state agencies and controlling notices rather than presenting a national rule as universally applicable.

## Advertising changes requiring review

Professional review is appropriate before:

- enabling personalized advertising;
- expanding advertising onto healthcare, Medicare, Medicaid, tax, retirement, career, medical-bill, or benefits decision routes;
- using contextual advertising beside urgent instructions;
- adding affiliate links or compensated rankings;
- receiving compensation from insurers, brokers, advisers, lenders, providers, hospitals, employers, or benefits vendors;
- sharing analytics identifiers for advertising purposes;
- changing consent behavior for EEA, United Kingdom, Swiss, California, Colorado, Connecticut, Virginia, or other regulated visitors;
- treating analytics or advertising disclosures as outside statutory definitions of sale or sharing without legal analysis.

Current sensitive routes should remain ad-free by default.

## Future sensitive-data systems requiring review

Do not implement the following without a separately approved architecture, threat model, data map, retention schedule, vendor review, security controls, and legal review:

- user accounts or cross-device synchronization;
- uploaded bills, EOBs, medical records, tax documents, pay statements, or benefits documents;
- OCR or document extraction;
- bank, brokerage, payroll, HR, insurer, provider, or government portal integrations;
- stored names, addresses, diagnoses, procedures, medication lists, provider identities, employer identities, claim numbers, account numbers, or government identifiers;
- email or SMS reminders containing financial, medical, insurance, employer, or benefits details;
- personalized recommendations based on stored financial or health profiles;
- automated appeal letters using case-specific facts;
- AI chat history retained as a user case file.

## Advice and determination boundaries that must remain explicit

The site must not claim to provide or make:

- individualized financial advice;
- investment advice or fiduciary services;
- tax advice or tax-return determinations;
- legal advice;
- insurance-agent or broker services;
- medical advice;
- official eligibility decisions;
- official coverage decisions;
- prior-authorization decisions;
- billing-liability decisions;
- coding or medical-necessity determinations;
- employment-law conclusions;
- plan-selection recommendations;
- appeal-outcome guarantees;
- guaranteed savings, approval, coverage, benefits, or investment returns.

A disclaimer does not cure misleading underlying content. Calculations, labels, result language, sources, and next steps must remain qualified and accurate.

## Remaining jurisdictional uncertainty

The website currently serves a broad public audience and may be accessible outside North Carolina and outside the United States. Applicability of privacy, consumer-protection, accessibility, advertising, professional-services, and data-transfer laws depends on facts including audience, traffic, data collected, vendors, monetization, business size, and jurisdiction.

The site should not make categorical legal-compliance claims based only on technical controls.

## Risks that disclaimers cannot eliminate

Disclaimers cannot eliminate:

- inaccurate or stale factual claims;
- misleading calculations;
- inaccessible interfaces;
- unlawful data collection or disclosure;
- failure to honor consent choices;
- deceptive advertising placement;
- copyright or trademark infringement;
- security vulnerabilities;
- failure to comply with applicable state or international law;
- statements that function as individualized professional advice despite a disclaimer;
- operational mismatch between policy language and actual vendor behavior.

## Recommended professional-review sequence

1. Terms of Use, Privacy Policy, Disclosures, and Advertising Policy.
2. State-specific Medicaid, billing, collections, and insurance content.
3. Analytics and advertising consent architecture.
4. Any proposed affiliate or sponsorship model.
5. Any account, upload, reminder, or synchronization product.
6. Any tool that moves from broad educational categories toward individual tax, investment, legal, insurance, or medical recommendations.

## Current technical safeguards

The current repository includes:

- explicit analytics consent controls;
- URL query and fragment minimization at the Google telemetry boundary;
- allowlisted categorical analytics properties;
- ad-free sensitive tools and workspaces;
- browser-local storage schemas with validation and reset controls;
- no user accounts;
- no sensitive document uploads;
- no bank, payroll, HR, insurer, or provider integrations;
- shared educational disclaimers;
- primary-source and freshness checks;
- build-blocking publication-readiness checks;
- qualified decision outputs;
- fixed-action My Plan storage rather than raw answer storage.

These controls reduce avoidable exposure but are not a substitute for legal review.
