# Page 1 — Start Here

## Direct answer

Medicaid is state-administered coverage and assistance under federal rules. The first step is finding the right application path for your state and situation.

## First check

- **State:** Medicaid is run by states, so the exact application path and rules can vary.
- **Household:** Eligibility can depend on household size and relationship rules.
- **Income:** Income rules can vary by state and category.
- **Category:** Children, pregnant people, adults, older adults, people with disabilities, and long-term care applicants may be reviewed differently.
- **Need:** Health coverage, nursing home care, home- and community-based services, and Medicare cost help are different questions.

## First move

Apply through HealthCare.gov/Marketplace or your state Medicaid agency, then respond quickly to document requests.

# Page 2 — Medicaid Is Not One Simple National Rule

## Direct answer

Medicaid is a federal-state program. That means the federal framework matters, but your state and eligibility category often control the practical answer.

## Watch out

- **State rules:** Eligibility, covered services, and application steps can vary.
- **Category:** A low-income adult, child, pregnant person, older adult, person with disability, or long-term care applicant may face different rules.
- **Documents:** Approval may depend on proof of identity, residency, income, household, and other facts.
- **Timing:** Medicaid and CHIP can generally be applied for year-round, but responses and deadlines still matter.
- **Managed care:** Some states use Medicaid managed care plans, which may have their own networks and processes.

## Better question

Ask: “What state program and eligibility category am I applying under, and what proof do they need?”

# Page 3 — Where to Apply

## Direct answer

Many people can apply through the Health Insurance Marketplace or directly through their state Medicaid agency. The right path depends on the state and situation.

## Compare

- **HealthCare.gov/Marketplace:** Can screen for Medicaid, CHIP, and Marketplace plan options.
- **State Medicaid agency:** Can provide state-specific application rules, notices, renewals, and case information.
- **Local help:** Navigators, state assistance programs, legal aid, and caseworkers may help with complicated situations.
- **Long-term care:** Nursing home or home-care Medicaid may require state-specific forms and additional financial review.

## Ask

- Should I apply through HealthCare.gov, my state Medicaid agency, or both?
- Is this regular Medicaid, CHIP, long-term care Medicaid, or Medicare cost help?
- What happens after I submit the application?

# Page 4 — What Medicaid May Help With

## Direct answer

Medicaid may help with health coverage and, for eligible people, long-term services and supports. The exact help depends on state rules and eligibility category.

## Sort the need

- **Health coverage:** Doctor visits, hospital care, prescriptions, and other covered services depending on state/program rules.
- **Children's coverage:** CHIP or Medicaid may apply depending on household and state rules.
- **Medicare help:** Some people with Medicare may qualify for Medicaid or Medicare Savings Programs.
- **Long-term services and supports:** Medicaid may help eligible people with nursing facility care or home- and community-based services.
- **Managed care:** If enrolled in a Medicaid managed care plan, network and plan processes may matter.

## Ask

- What program am I applying for?
- What services are covered in my state?
- Is the provider, facility, agency, or plan part of the Medicaid network?

# Page 5 — Documents to Gather

## Direct answer

A Medicaid application usually moves faster when the applicant has proof ready. The exact document list depends on the state and eligibility category.

## Gather

- **Identity:** Name, date of birth, Social Security number if applicable, and identity documents.
- **Residency:** State residency and contact information.
- **Household:** Household members and relationships.
- **Income:** Pay, Social Security, pension, unemployment, self-employment, or other income information.
- **Coverage:** Existing health insurance, Medicare, employer coverage, or Marketplace coverage.
- **Long-term care:** Bank, asset, transfer, facility, and care-need records may be requested for some applications.

## Ask

- What documents are required for this exact Medicaid category?
- What deadline applies to document requests?
- How do I upload, mail, fax, or bring proof?

# Page 6 — Applying for Someone Else

## Direct answer

Helping a parent or family member apply may require permission, identity verification, representative paperwork, or facility/social-work coordination.

## Verify

- **Permission:** Ask what authorization is needed to speak or act for the applicant.
- **Contact person:** Confirm where notices will be sent.
- **Deadline owner:** Decide who is responsible for responding to requests.
- **Facility role:** If a hospital, nursing facility, or social worker is helping, clarify what they will and will not submit.
- **Legal authority:** Power of attorney, guardianship, or authorized representative rules may matter.

## Ask

- Can I receive notices or speak for the applicant?
- What form authorizes me to help?
- Who gets document requests and deadlines?

# Page 7 — Medicaid and Long-Term Care

## Direct answer

Long-term care Medicaid is often different from applying for basic health coverage. It can involve medical need, financial eligibility, facility rules, and state-specific review.

## Watch out

- **Custodial care:** Daily living help is often the long-term care issue families are trying to solve.
- **Nursing facility care:** State Medicaid rules may apply after eligibility review.
- **Home services:** Home- and community-based services may have state-specific programs or waitlists.
- **Assets:** Some long-term care applications review assets and transfers.
- **Estate recovery:** Medicaid estate recovery rules can matter and vary by state.
- **Spousal rules:** Spousal impoverishment protections may apply in some cases.

## Ask

- Is this regular Medicaid, long-term care Medicaid, or a waiver/home-care program?
- What financial records are required?
- Should we contact an elder-law attorney or state Medicaid specialist before signing anything?

# Page 8 — After You Apply

## Direct answer

Submitting the application is not the finish line. Watch for notices, document requests, approval, denial, renewal, and plan-selection steps.

## Timeline

- **Submit:** Save confirmation details.
- **Request for proof:** Respond by the deadline.
- **Decision:** Read the notice carefully.
- **Approval:** Ask what coverage starts, what plan applies, and what card or notice arrives next.
- **Denial:** Check why, what proof is missing, and whether appeal or reconsideration is available.
- **Renewal:** Medicaid eligibility may need to be renewed or updated.

## Ask

- How will I receive notices?
- What is the case number?
- What deadline applies to proof, appeal, or renewal?

# Page 9 — If You Are Denied or Lose Medicaid

## Direct answer

A denial or loss of Medicaid is not the same as the end of all options. The next step is to identify the reason, deadline, and alternative coverage path.

## Check

- **Reason:** Income, documents, residency, category, assets, renewal, or missing information.
- **Deadline:** Appeal, reconsideration, or proof deadlines can be time-sensitive.
- **Marketplace:** Denial or loss of Medicaid may connect to Marketplace coverage options.
- **Medicare:** If the person has Medicare, Medicare Savings Programs or other help may still be relevant.
- **State help:** State Medicaid agencies, navigators, legal aid, or SHIP may help depending on the issue.

## Ask

- Why was Medicaid denied or ended?
- Can missing proof fix the issue?
- What is the appeal or review deadline?
- What coverage option should we check next?

# Page 10 — Scripts and Next Steps

## Direct answer

Use short questions, keep records, and do not rely on verbal answers alone when a deadline, denial, facility bill, or long-term care decision is involved.

## Scripts

- “Which Medicaid program or category is this application under?”
- “What documents are still missing?”
- “What is the deadline to send proof?”
- “How do I become an authorized representative?”
- “Is this health coverage, long-term care Medicaid, CHIP, or Medicare cost help?”
- “If denied, what is the appeal or review process?”
- “Can you send that answer in writing?”

## Next step

Apply through HealthCare.gov or your state Medicaid agency, then save every confirmation, notice, upload receipt, and deadline.
