# Chapter 1 — Who This Guide Is For

## Direct answer

This guide is for patients, caregivers, adult children, spouses, and families trying to understand Medicare, Medicaid, rehab, home health, skilled nursing facility care, long-term care, and confusing medical bills.

## Plain-English explanation

Families often meet Medicare and Medicaid rules during stressful moments: a parent is hospitalized, a spouse is being discharged, a patient is choosing rehab, or a bill arrives that does not make sense.

This guide is designed to slow the situation down. It will not decide the case for the reader. It will help the reader understand the next question to ask, who to ask, and which document or source may verify the answer.

## Common misunderstanding

The biggest misunderstanding is: "The patient needs care, so Medicare must pay for it." Need and coverage are related, but they are not the same.

## Hospital/caregiver example

A parent falls, goes to the hospital, and the family hears the word "rehab." The family assumes Medicare will automatically cover the stay. The better first question is: what skilled care is being ordered, what hospital status applies, what facility is being used, and what payer rules must be met?

## Questions to ask

- What problem are we solving: discharge, rehab, home health, long-term care, Medicaid, or a bill?
- Which coverage is involved: Original Medicare, Medicare Advantage, Medicaid, Medigap, retiree coverage, or another plan?
- Who can verify the answer in writing?
- What document should we ask for before making a decision?
