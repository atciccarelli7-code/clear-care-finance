# Chapter 2 — Disclaimer and Boundaries

## Direct answer

This guide teaches concepts and questions. It does not determine coverage, eligibility, medical necessity, discharge safety, legal strategy, tax treatment, insurance selection, or financial planning decisions for a specific person.

## Plain-English explanation

Medicare and Medicaid decisions can depend on details: state, plan, provider, facility, diagnosis, timing, documentation, prior authorization, income, resources, and the exact service being requested.

A plain-English guide can help a family ask better questions. It cannot replace official sources, plan documents, billing offices, clinicians, SHIP counselors, attorneys, licensed insurance professionals, or other qualified professionals.

## Common misunderstanding

A family may read a general rule and assume it applies exactly to their situation. General education should lead to better verification, not replace verification.

## Hospital/caregiver example

A caregiver reads that Medicare generally does not pay for most long-term custodial care. That is an important starting point, but the family still needs to verify whether the patient has a skilled need, Medicare Advantage rules, Medicaid eligibility, state LTSS options, or other coverage.

## Questions to ask

- Which official source applies to this situation?
- Is this Original Medicare, Medicare Advantage, Medicaid, or another payer?
- Is there a written notice, denial, authorization, bill, EOB, Medicare Summary Notice, or plan document?
- Should we contact SHIP, the state Medicaid agency, the plan, the facility, the billing office, or a qualified professional?
