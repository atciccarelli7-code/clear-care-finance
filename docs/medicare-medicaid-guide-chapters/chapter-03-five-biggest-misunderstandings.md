# Chapter 3 — The 5 Biggest Misunderstandings

## Direct answer

The five biggest misunderstandings are: Medicare and Medicaid are different; covered does not always mean free; skilled care is different from custodial care; sleeping in a hospital bed does not automatically mean inpatient status; and Medicare Advantage can involve networks and prior authorization.

## Plain-English explanation

These five points explain much of the confusion families face around hospitalization and discharge. A person can be sick and still run into coverage rules. A service can be covered and still leave a bill. A rehab stay can be recommended but still require documentation, facility rules, network status, and authorization.

The goal is not to memorize every rule. The goal is to recognize which question matters next.

## Common misunderstanding

Families often think one insurance card answers every question. In reality, Medicare, Medicaid, Medigap, Medicare Advantage, Part D, hospital billing, facility billing, and state rules may all touch different parts of one episode of care.

## Hospital/caregiver example

A patient leaves the hospital, enters rehab, improves slowly, and still cannot bathe or dress alone. Medicare may have covered part of the skilled rehab stay. That does not mean Medicare will pay indefinitely for daily custodial help.

## Questions to ask

- Is this Medicare or Medicaid?
- Is this skilled care or custodial care?
- Is the patient inpatient, outpatient, or observation?
- Is the provider or facility in-network or Medicare-certified?
- Is prior authorization required?
- What could the patient owe?
