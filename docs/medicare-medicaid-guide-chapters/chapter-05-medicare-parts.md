# Chapter 5 — Medicare Parts A, B, C, D, and Medigap

## Direct answer

Medicare has parts. Part A is commonly associated with inpatient hospital and certain post-hospital care. Part B covers many outpatient and physician services. Part C is Medicare Advantage. Part D is drug coverage. Medigap is supplemental coverage used with Original Medicare, not Medicare Advantage.

## Plain-English explanation

A Medicare card does not tell the whole story. The first question is how the person receives Medicare coverage.

Original Medicare includes Part A and Part B. A person with Original Medicare may also have Part D drug coverage and a Medigap policy. Medicare Advantage is another way to receive Medicare benefits through a private plan approved by Medicare. Medicare Advantage plans may include drug coverage and may use networks, referrals, and prior authorization.

## Common misunderstanding

People often ask, "Does Medicare cover this?" The better question is, "Which Medicare part, plan, provider, facility, pharmacy, and rule applies?"

## Hospital/caregiver example

One patient has Original Medicare, a Part D drug plan, and Medigap. Another patient has Medicare Advantage with drug coverage included. They both have Medicare, but their provider access, bills, authorizations, and drug costs may work differently.

## Questions to ask

- Does the patient have Original Medicare or Medicare Advantage?
- Does the patient have Part D drug coverage?
- Does the patient have Medigap, Medicaid, employer retiree coverage, or another supplement?
- Which card should be used for this service?
- Are there network or authorization rules?
