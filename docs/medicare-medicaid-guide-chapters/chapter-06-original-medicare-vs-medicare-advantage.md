# Chapter 6 — Original Medicare vs Medicare Advantage

## Direct answer

Original Medicare and Medicare Advantage are two different ways to receive Medicare coverage. Original Medicare generally offers broad access to Medicare-accepting providers, while Medicare Advantage often uses plan networks, plan cost-sharing, and prior authorization rules.

## Plain-English explanation

Original Medicare is run through the federal Medicare program. Medicare Advantage is offered by private plans approved by Medicare. Medicare Advantage plans must cover almost all medically necessary services Original Medicare covers, but they can use plan rules such as networks, referrals, and prior authorization.

Original Medicare usually does not have a built-in annual out-of-pocket maximum unless the person has supplemental coverage. Medicare Advantage plans have yearly limits for covered Medicare services, but costs, networks, drugs, and authorization rules vary by plan.

## Common misunderstanding

A low or zero premium does not mean a plan has zero medical cost risk. Premium is only one part of the total cost.

## Hospital/caregiver example

A patient with Original Medicare may be able to use any hospital or doctor that accepts Medicare. A patient with Medicare Advantage may need an in-network facility for non-emergency care and may need authorization before rehab or certain services.

## Questions to ask

- Is this Original Medicare or Medicare Advantage?
- Is the provider or facility in-network?
- Is prior authorization required?
- What is the maximum out-of-pocket limit for covered Medicare services?
- Are drugs included, or is there a separate Part D plan?
- Can the patient use Medigap with this coverage arrangement?
