# Chapter 8 — Why Medicare Can Pay and You Still Owe

## Direct answer

Medicare payment does not always mean the patient's bill is zero. Premiums, deductibles, copays, coinsurance, drug costs, non-covered services, provider status, and plan rules can still create patient responsibility.

## Plain-English explanation

Insurance payment and full payment are not the same. Medicare or a plan may approve a service and pay its share. The patient may still owe a deductible, coinsurance, a fixed copay, a daily facility amount, or the cost of services that were not covered.

Separate providers can also bill separately for the same episode of care. A hospital visit may involve the facility, physician group, lab, radiology, ambulance, anesthesia, or other professionals.

## Common misunderstanding

Patients sometimes assume a bill is wrong simply because Medicare already paid something. The bill may be wrong, but it may also reflect normal cost-sharing. Compare the bill against the Medicare Summary Notice or EOB before paying.

## Hospital/caregiver example

A patient receives an outpatient service covered under Part B. Medicare approves the claim and pays its share. The patient still owes coinsurance after the deductible because covered does not always mean free.

## Questions to ask

- Is this a premium, deductible, copay, coinsurance, non-covered charge, or out-of-network issue?
- Does the bill match the Medicare Summary Notice or EOB?
- What is the allowed amount?
- Did the provider accept assignment or participate in the plan network?
- Is financial assistance available?
