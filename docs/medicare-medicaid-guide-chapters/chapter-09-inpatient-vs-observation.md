# Chapter 9 — Inpatient vs Observation

## Direct answer

A patient can stay overnight in a hospital and still not be formally admitted as an inpatient. Observation time may not count the same way for some Medicare coverage rules, including certain skilled nursing facility coverage rules.

## Plain-English explanation

Hospital status is not just about where the patient sleeps. It is a billing and coverage classification. For some Medicare rules, especially skilled nursing facility coverage under Original Medicare, inpatient status and timing can matter.

Families should ask the hospital directly: is the patient inpatient, outpatient, or observation?

## Common misunderstanding

Families often think, "They stayed three nights, so rehab is covered." The more precise question is whether those nights counted as qualifying inpatient days under the applicable rule.

## Hospital/caregiver example

A patient spends one night in the emergency department, one night under observation, and then is admitted inpatient. The family should not assume all nights count the same way toward skilled nursing facility coverage.

## Questions to ask

- What is the patient's official hospital status right now?
- When did inpatient admission begin, if it began?
- Does observation time count for the next service being requested?
- Has the hospital provided any required notice?
- How does the Medicare Advantage plan handle this, if applicable?
