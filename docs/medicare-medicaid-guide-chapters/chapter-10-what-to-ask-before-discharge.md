# Chapter 10 — What to Ask Before Discharge

## Direct answer

Before discharge, families should ask where the patient is going, what care is needed, who is expected to pay, what authorization is required, what the patient may owe, and what the backup plan is if coverage is denied or ends.

## Plain-English explanation

Discharge can move quickly. Families may hear terms like rehab, home health, walker, oxygen, therapy, skilled nursing, or facility placement without understanding the coverage path.

Asking early is better than trying to fix confusion after the patient is already moved. The goal is not to challenge every recommendation. The goal is to understand the plan, the payer rules, and the financial exposure.

## Common misunderstanding

Families may think discharge planning is only about where the patient goes. It is also about payer rules, documentation, equipment, medications, transportation, follow-up appointments, and safety.

## Hospital/caregiver example

A patient is recommended for skilled rehab. The family asks whether the facility is Medicare-certified or in-network, whether authorization is approved, what the daily cost could be, and what happens if coverage stops before the patient is independent.

## Questions to ask

- What is the recommended discharge setting?
- What skilled need is documented?
- What services are ordered?
- Which payer is expected to pay?
- Is authorization approved?
- What could the patient owe?
- What happens if coverage is denied or ends?
- What is the backup plan if home is not safe?
