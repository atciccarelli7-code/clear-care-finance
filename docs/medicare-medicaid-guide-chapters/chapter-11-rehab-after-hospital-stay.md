# Chapter 11 — Rehab After a Hospital Stay

## Direct answer

Medicare may cover short-term skilled rehab after a hospital stay when rules are met, but coverage is not automatic. The patient usually needs skilled care, the facility must meet requirements, and plan rules may apply.

## Plain-English explanation

Rehab is often where families first discover the difference between needing help and meeting coverage rules. Original Medicare has specific requirements for skilled nursing facility coverage. Medicare Advantage plans can apply network and prior authorization rules and may operate differently from Original Medicare.

The word "rehab" can mean different things depending on the setting, payer, and service. Families should ask what type of rehab is being recommended and which payer rules apply.

## Common misunderstanding

A doctor, therapist, or hospital recommending rehab does not guarantee payment. The payer still applies coverage rules.

## Hospital/caregiver example

A patient leaves the hospital weak after pneumonia. Therapy recommends skilled nursing facility rehab. The family should ask about hospital status, skilled need, facility certification, authorization, network status, daily cost, and coverage-ending criteria.

## Questions to ask

- What skilled service is being ordered?
- Is the recommended setting SNF, inpatient rehab, home health, or outpatient therapy?
- Is the facility Medicare-certified or in-network?
- Has authorization been approved?
- What days or cost-sharing might apply?
- What happens if the patient still needs help after rehab ends?
