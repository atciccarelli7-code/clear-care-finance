# Chapter 12 — Skilled Nursing Facility Care

## Direct answer

Skilled nursing facility care is short-term care that may be covered by Medicare Part A for eligible patients when specific rules are met. It is not the same as long-term custodial nursing home care.

## Plain-English explanation

A skilled nursing facility can provide nursing care, therapy, medications, medical social services, meals, and other covered services during a qualifying stay. The key word is skilled. The care must require professional nursing or therapy skills and meet coverage rules.

Under Original Medicare, SNF coverage is limited within a benefit period and can involve daily coinsurance after the early covered days. Medicare Advantage plans may apply different operational rules, including authorization and network requirements.

## Common misunderstanding

Families hear "nursing facility" and assume all nursing home care is Medicare-covered. Medicare-covered SNF care is limited and short-term. Long-term custodial care is different.

## Hospital/caregiver example

A patient needs daily physical therapy and skilled nursing after surgery. A SNF stay may be covered if requirements are met. Later, if the patient only needs help with meals, bathing, dressing, and supervision, the coverage question changes.

## Questions to ask

- Is the patient receiving daily skilled care?
- Is the facility Medicare-certified or in-network?
- How many days are expected to be covered?
- What is the daily cost-sharing after the early covered days?
- What signs could cause coverage to end?
- What is the plan if the patient still needs help after skilled care ends?
