# Chapter 17 — Medicare Advantage Prior Authorization

## Direct answer

Prior authorization means a plan may require approval before it agrees to cover a service, facility stay, equipment, drug, or treatment. Medicare Advantage plans may require prior authorization for some services and supplies.

## Plain-English explanation

Prior authorization is not the same as a clinician's recommendation. A clinician may recommend care, but the plan may still require documentation and approval before payment. Delays, denials, and appeals can happen.

Families should document dates, reference numbers, who submitted the request, what was requested, whether the request was approved or denied, and any deadline to appeal.

## Common misunderstanding

Families often think, "The doctor ordered it, so the plan approved it." Those are separate steps.

## Hospital/caregiver example

A patient needs rehab after hospitalization. The facility and care team recommend placement, but the Medicare Advantage plan must authorize the stay. The family should ask whether authorization has been submitted, approved, denied, or is still pending.

## Questions to ask

- Is prior authorization required?
- Who submitted it?
- When was it submitted?
- What is the reference number?
- Is it approved, denied, pending, or partially approved?
- What is the appeal deadline if denied?
- What documentation does the plan say is missing?
