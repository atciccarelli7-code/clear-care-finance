# Chapter 18 — Bills, EOBs, Medicare Summary Notices, and Scripts

## Direct answer

Before paying a confusing bill, compare the provider bill to the Medicare Summary Notice or Explanation of Benefits. Confirm the date of service, provider, allowed amount, plan payment, and patient responsibility.

## Plain-English explanation

One hospital visit can create multiple bills. A facility may bill separately from physicians, radiology, lab, ambulance, anesthesia, therapy, or outside professionals. The first bill is not always the full story, and it is not always correct.

The goal is not to avoid legitimate bills. The goal is to understand what the bill represents before paying.

## Common misunderstanding

Patients may believe one hospital bill includes everything. Many medical bills come from separate legal entities or provider groups.

## Hospital/caregiver example

A patient receives a hospital facility bill and a separate physician bill for the same date. The family checks both against the EOB or Medicare Summary Notice and asks why each balance is listed as patient responsibility.

## Questions to ask

- What date of service is this?
- Which provider or facility is billing me?
- Has Medicare or the plan processed this claim?
- What was the allowed amount?
- What did the plan pay?
- Why is this my responsibility?
- Is financial assistance available?
- Should this be appealed, corrected, or rebilled?
