# Chapter 19 — Worksheets, QR Codes, Sources, and Update Log

## Direct answer

The final guide should include practical worksheets and QR codes so families can move from reading to action.

## Plain-English explanation

A useful guide should not end with a long explanation. It should help the reader organize the next phone call, discharge meeting, facility comparison, bill review, or Medicaid question.

The worksheets should be simple enough to print, mark up, and bring to a discharge conversation or billing call.

## Common misunderstanding

Families often try to keep every detail in their head during a crisis. Written notes, reference numbers, dates, and names matter.

## Hospital/caregiver example

A daughter is trying to compare two rehab facilities while also calling the Medicare Advantage plan and tracking a bill. A worksheet gives her one place to write the facility name, authorization status, expected daily cost, network status, and what happens if coverage ends.

## Questions to ask

- What decision are we trying to organize?
- What phone numbers, names, dates, and reference numbers should be written down?
- Which QR code sends the reader to the most relevant tool?
- Has the source or dollar amount been updated recently?
- Does the worksheet preserve educational boundaries?
