## Facebook post 3 — Medicare vs Medicaid

One of the biggest family misunderstandings:

Medicare and Medicaid are not the same thing.

Medicare is generally health insurance tied to age, disability, or certain conditions. Medicaid is state-administered assistance that may become especially important for long-term services and supports if someone qualifies.

That distinction matters when a family is trying to understand rehab, nursing home care, home support, and long-term care costs.

Plain-English guide landing page:
`https://communityacquiredfinance.com/guides/medicare-medicaid-rehab-long-term-care`

## Facebook post 4 — Long-term care misconception

A patient can truly need help and still run into Medicare coverage limits.

That is one of the hardest parts for families to understand. Needing help with bathing, dressing, meals, toileting, supervision, or transportation is real. But Medicare does not pay for most long-term custodial care.

That is why families need to know when Medicaid, state programs, private pay, family caregiving, or professional guidance may become part of the conversation.

I’m building a guide to help families ask better questions:
`https://communityacquiredfinance.com/guides/medicare-medicaid-rehab-long-term-care`

## Facebook post 5 — Bills after Medicare paid

Medicare can pay and the patient can still owe money.

That does not automatically mean the bill is wrong. It may be a deductible, coinsurance, copay, non-covered service, separate provider bill, or plan rule.

But it also does not mean you should pay a confusing bill blindly.

Before paying, compare the bill to the Medicare Summary Notice or Explanation of Benefits and ask the billing office to explain the patient responsibility.

## Facebook post 9 — Myth post

Myth: "If Medicare covers it, it is free."

Reality: covered services can still involve premiums, deductibles, copays, coinsurance, daily facility costs, or non-covered pieces.

A better question is: what did Medicare or the plan approve, what did it pay, and why is this amount listed as patient responsibility?

# Language to avoid

Do not use:

- "Guaranteed savings"
- "Secrets Medicare does not want you to know"
- "Avoid paying nursing home bills"
- "Best Medicare plan"
- "We can get you approved"
- "Apply now"
- "Talk to an agent"
- "Beat Medicare"
- "Loopholes"
- "Free care"
- "Hospital-approved"
- "Medicare-approved guide"

Use:

- "plain-English"
- "educational"
- "questions to ask"
- "verify your situation"
- "source review"
- "patients, caregivers, and families"
- "not affiliated or endorsed"
