## Fact-check standard

Claim status definitions:

- **Supported**: directly supported by official sources in the source binder.
- **Supported with wording caution**: generally supported, but final wording must avoid overclaiming or add context.
- **Source gap closed, still verify before release**: an official source anchor has been added, but final editorial review should still check exact wording, timing, and dollar amounts.
- **Needs local/state verification**: depends on state Medicaid, plan, county, facility, or personal facts.
- **Unsupported / remove or revise**: not adequately supported by official sources.

## High-confidence supported claims

These core claims remain supported and should remain central to the guide:

1. Original Medicare includes Part A and Part B.
2. People can receive Medicare coverage through Original Medicare or Medicare Advantage.
3. Original Medicare covers many approved services and supplies, but not all costs.
4. Original Medicare has no annual out-of-pocket limit unless the person has other coverage such as Medigap, Medicaid, employer, retiree, or union coverage.
5. Original Medicare generally allows use of doctors and hospitals that take Medicare, with assignment/cost caveats.
6. Medicare Advantage bundles Part A, Part B, and usually Part D.
7. Medicare Advantage plans may use networks and prior authorization for some services or supplies.
8. Medigap can be used with Original Medicare, but not to pay Medicare Advantage out-of-pocket costs.
9. Medicare does not pay for most long-term custodial care.
10. Long-term care is different from short-term skilled nursing facility care.
11. Medicaid is the primary payer across the country for long-term care services.
12. Medicaid LTSS can involve institutional and community-based settings.
13. Medicaid rules are state-administered and state-specific verification is required.
14. Medicare Part A covers skilled nursing facility care for eligible beneficiaries for a limited time when rules are met.
15. Original Medicare generally requires a qualifying inpatient hospital stay of at least 3 days in a row for SNF coverage, not counting discharge day.
16. Observation or emergency room time before inpatient admission does not count toward the 3-day qualifying inpatient stay for Original Medicare SNF coverage.
17. Medicare Advantage plans may require prior authorization for some services or supplies.
18. A Medicare Summary Notice is not a bill and can be used as a comparison document for Original Medicare claims.
19. Medicare Savings Programs may help eligible people with certain Medicare costs, and the state determines qualification.
20. QMB has specific Medicare-covered cost-sharing billing protections, but the person's exact status must be verified.
21. Medicare and Medicare Advantage decisions can have appeal processes and written deadlines.
22. Estate recovery and spousal impoverishment are Medicaid topics that should be treated as caution-only questions, not DIY legal planning instructions.
