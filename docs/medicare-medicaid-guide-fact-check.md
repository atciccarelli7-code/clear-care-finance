## Fact-check standard

Claim status definitions:

- **Supported**: directly supported by an official source in the source binder or by official source review.
- **Supported with wording caution**: generally supported, but final wording should avoid overclaiming or should add context.
- **Needs added source**: likely accurate, but the current binder does not yet include the best official source.
- **Needs local/state verification**: depends on state Medicaid, plan, county, facility, or personal facts.
- **Unsupported / remove or revise**: not adequately supported by the current official-source set.

## High-confidence supported claims

These core claims are supported and should remain central to the guide.

1. **Original Medicare includes Part A and Part B.**
   - Status: Supported.
   - Source note: Medicare.gov How Medicare Works.

2. **People can receive Medicare coverage through Original Medicare or Medicare Advantage.**
   - Status: Supported.
   - Source note: Medicare.gov How Medicare Works.

3. **Original Medicare covers many approved services and supplies, but not all costs.**
   - Status: Supported.
   - Source note: Medicare.gov How Medicare Works.

4. **Original Medicare has no annual out-of-pocket limit unless the person has other coverage, such as Medigap, Medicaid, employer, retiree, or union coverage.**
   - Status: Supported.
   - Source note: Medicare.gov How Medicare Works; Medicare.gov Compare Original Medicare and Medicare Advantage; Medicare.gov Costs.

5. **Original Medicare generally lets people use any doctor or hospital that takes Medicare in the United States.**
   - Status: Supported with wording caution.
   - Source note: Medicare.gov Compare Original Medicare and Medicare Advantage.
   - Wording caution: Include the Medicare.gov caveat that costs may be higher if the provider does not accept assignment.

6. **Medicare Advantage bundles Part A, Part B, and usually Part D.**
   - Status: Supported.
   - Source note: Medicare.gov How Medicare Works.

7. **Medicare Advantage plans may require network use for non-emergency care.**
   - Status: Supported.
   - Source note: Medicare.gov How Medicare Works; Compare Original Medicare and Medicare Advantage.

8. **Medicare Advantage plans may require prior authorization for some services or supplies.**
   - Status: Supported.
   - Source note: Medicare.gov Compare Original Medicare and Medicare Advantage.

9. **Medigap can be used with Original Medicare but cannot be bought to cover Medicare Advantage out-of-pocket costs.**
   - Status: Supported.
   - Source note: Medicare.gov How Medicare Works; Compare Original Medicare and Medicare Advantage.

10. **Medicare does not pay for long-term care.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Long-Term Care.

11. **Long-term care is also called custodial care or long-term services and supports.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Long-Term Care.

12. **Most long-term care helps with activities of daily living such as dressing, bathing, bathroom use, home-delivered meals, adult day health care, and transportation.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Long-Term Care.

13. **Long-term care is different from skilled nursing facility care.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Long-Term Care; Medicare.gov Skilled Nursing Facility Care.

14. **Medicare and most health insurance, including Medigap, do not pay for long-term care services.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Long-Term Care.

15. **People may be eligible for long-term care through Medicaid if they meet state eligibility requirements.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Long-Term Care; Medicaid.gov Eligibility Policy.

16. **Medicaid is the primary payer across the nation for long-term care services.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: Medicaid.gov Long-Term Services and Supports.

17. **Medicaid LTSS can cover services across institutional and community-based settings.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: Medicaid.gov Long-Term Services and Supports.

18. **Medicaid rules are state-administered and state-specific verification is required.**
    - Status: Supported with wording caution.
    - Source note: Medicaid.gov Eligibility Policy; state Medicaid agencies.
    - Wording caution: Avoid implying all Medicaid LTSS details are identical across states.

19. **Medicare Part A covers skilled nursing facility care for eligible beneficiaries for a limited time on a short-term basis.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Skilled Nursing Facility Care.

20. **Skilled care is nursing or therapy care that requires professional or technical personnel or their supervision.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Skilled Nursing Facility Care.

21. **Original Medicare generally requires a qualifying inpatient hospital stay of at least 3 days in a row for SNF coverage, not counting the discharge day.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Skilled Nursing Facility Care.

22. **Observation or emergency room time before inpatient admission does not count toward the 3-day qualifying inpatient stay, even if the patient was there overnight.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Skilled Nursing Facility Care.

23. **Medicare Advantage plans may waive the 3-day minimum inpatient hospital stay.**
    - Status: Supported with wording caution.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Skilled Nursing Facility Care.
    - Wording caution: Say the patient should contact the plan for details; do not imply all plans waive it.

24. **2026 Part A inpatient hospital deductible is $1,736 per benefit period.**
    - Status: Supported for 2026.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Costs.
    - Wording caution: Must be rechecked before final publication and updated annually.

25. **2026 SNF cost sharing is $0 for days 1-20, $217/day for days 21-100, and all costs after day 100, with benefit-period context.**
    - Status: Supported for 2026.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Costs; Medicare.gov Skilled Nursing Facility Care.
    - Wording caution: Include Part A deductible / benefit-period context and Medicare Advantage plan variation.

26. **2026 Part B premium is $202.90 for most people and the Part B deductible is $283.**
    - Status: Supported for 2026.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Costs.
    - Wording caution: Higher-income amounts and penalties can apply; must be rechecked before publication.

27. **Part B-covered services usually involve 20% coinsurance after the deductible when the provider accepts assignment.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Costs.

28. **Medicare covers certain home health services under Part A and Part B if eligibility rules are met.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Home Health Services.

29. **Medicare does not pay for 24-hour-a-day home care, home meal delivery, unrelated homemaker services, or custodial care when that is the only care needed.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Home Health Services.

30. **Home health eligibility includes needing part-time or intermittent skilled services and being homebound.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Home Health Services.

31. **Medicare-covered home health services cost $0, while covered DME generally involves 20% coinsurance after the Part B deductible.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Home Health Services; Medicare.gov Costs.

32. **Medicare Part B covers medically necessary DME if eligibility rules are met.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Durable Medical Equipment.

33. **DME must be durable, used for a medical reason, typically useful only to someone sick or injured, used in the home, and expected to last at least 3 years.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Durable Medical Equipment.

34. **DME supplier participation and assignment can affect what the patient owes.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: Medicare.gov Durable Medical Equipment.

35. **Deductible, copayment, coinsurance, out-of-pocket maximum, and allowed amount definitions are supported by HealthCare.gov glossary sources.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: HealthCare.gov glossary pages.

36. **SHIP provides local, one-on-one Medicare counseling and can help users find local Medicare help by state or territory.**
    - Status: Supported.
    - Source note: SHIP Help.

## Fact-check conclusion

The guide's core factual foundation is strong. The highest-value claims are supported: Medicare and Medicaid are different, Medicare does not pay for long-term care, SNF care is limited and rule-dependent, observation status can matter for SNF coverage, home health is not 24-hour custodial care, Medicare Advantage may involve networks and prior authorization, and patients can owe money even when Medicare or a plan pays.

The draft should not be released as a final PDF yet. It needs added official sources for dual eligibility, observation notices, appeals, Medicare Summary Notices, EOB/billing explanations, and state Medicaid topics. After those source gaps are closed, the manuscript can move into a final editorial pass and then print/PDF design.
