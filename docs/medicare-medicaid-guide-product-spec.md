# Medicare and Medicaid Flagship Guide Product Spec

Community Acquired Finance — Phase 1 specification.  
Last updated: 2026-07-06.

## Product

Create a standalone flagship guide: **The Hospital Family Guide to Medicare, Medicaid, Rehab, and Long-Term Care**.

The guide should help patients, caregivers, adult children, families, and healthcare workers understand common coverage and cost questions around hospital discharge, rehab, skilled nursing facility care, home health, long-term care, Medicaid, dual eligibility, Medicare Advantage prior authorization, and bills after Medicare payment.

This is not the full guide. This is the product spec only.

## 1. Title options

- The Hospital Family Guide to Medicare, Medicaid, Rehab, and Long-Term Care
- The Plain-English Medicare and Medicaid Guide for Patients, Caregivers, and Families
- Before Discharge: A Family Guide to Medicare, Medicaid, Rehab, and Long-Term Care
- Medicare, Medicaid, and Hospital Discharge: A Plain-English Guide for Families

Campaign phrase: **Before discharge, ask better questions.**

## 2. Target audience

Primary: caregivers, adult children, spouses, patients on Medicare, and families facing discharge, rehab, nursing home, home health, or bill confusion.

Secondary: healthcare workers, senior centers, caregiver groups, libraries, churches, and community groups that need a calm educational resource.

## 3. Primary user problems

- Medicare and Medicaid are easy to confuse.
- Families often assume Medicare covers most long-term care.
- Skilled care and custodial care sound similar but can lead to very different payment outcomes.
- Families often do not know what to ask before discharge.
- Rehab coverage can depend on status, skilled need, facility rules, plan rules, and authorization.
- Medicare Advantage networks and prior authorization can surprise families.
- Patients may still owe money even after Medicare or a plan pays.

## 4. Differentiation

Official sites explain rules. This guide should translate rules into practical questions families can ask during hospitalization, discharge planning, rehab placement, long-term care discussions, and billing confusion.

It should not rank insurers, recommend plans, collect leads, or sound like sales copy.

## 5. Recommended format

Final package:

- Main 45-70 page PDF guide.
- Two-page quick-start handout.
- One-page QR flyer.
- Website landing page.
- Social media distribution package.

## 7. Chapter purpose

Every chapter should answer one practical question, define the confusing terms, explain what families often misunderstand, give a realistic example, list questions to ask, link to a website tool or article, and show sources.

## 8. Required source categories

Use Medicare.gov, CMS.gov, Medicaid.gov, Healthcare.gov, SSA.gov when relevant, official state Medicaid pages, SHIP resources, state long-term care ombudsman resources, KFF, MACPAC, and MedPAC when useful.

Avoid low-quality SEO blogs, unsourced summaries, sales pages as primary evidence, and copied competitor explanations.

## 10. Worksheets

Include: before-discharge checklist, rehab comparison worksheet, skilled vs custodial care worksheet, prior authorization tracker, bill review worksheet, Medicaid/LTSS next-step worksheet, and caregiver document checklist.

## 11. Landing page

The landing page should include title, promise, who it helps, download CTA, disclaimer, source transparency, last updated date, related calculators, and related articles. No ad-first layout, affiliate framing, insurer rankings, or aggressive email capture.

## 12. QR flyer

The flyer should say: **Confused about Medicare, Medicaid, rehab, or nursing home costs?**

It should point to the guide landing page and clearly state that the guide is educational and not endorsed by a hospital, insurer, Medicare, Medicaid, CMS, or employer.

## 13. Social package

Create Facebook posts, LinkedIn posts, caregiver captions, healthcare-worker captions, and carousel scripts around discharge questions, Medicare vs Medicaid, skilled vs custodial care, rehab coverage, and bills after Medicare payment.

## 14. Trust language

State clearly that the guide is educational only and does not replace official sources, plan documents, billing offices, clinicians, SHIP counselors, or qualified professionals. Rules can vary by state, plan, facility, timing, and personal circumstances.

Author positioning may say: **Written from a healthcare-worker perspective by Andrew Ciccarelli, RN, BSN.**

## 15. Out of scope

Do not include personalized benefits determinations, state-specific eligibility calculations, plan rankings, insurer recommendations, enrollment recommendations, affiliate links, lead forms, facility rankings, clinical discharge recommendations, or political arguments.

## 16. Future expansion

Future versions can include state-specific Medicaid/LTSS guides, a SHIP and Medicaid contact directory, a healthcare-worker quick guide, a hospital bill workbook, a caregiver checklist, and a community workshop deck.

## 17. Acceptance criteria

The final guide must be useful, calm, printable, source-backed, mobile-readable, shareable, and mission-aligned. A family should be able to use it during a real discharge or billing problem. A healthcare worker should be able to share it without it feeling like sales material.
