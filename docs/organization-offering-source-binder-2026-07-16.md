**Reviewed:** July 16, 2026
**Purpose:** Record the authoritative standards and official competitor product pages used to define the public organization offering. Links should be rechecked before material legal, privacy, accessibility, product, or market claims change.

## Public-interest and risk standards

| Source | Use in the offering | Important boundary |
|---|---|---|
| [CDC: What Is Health Literacy?](https://www.cdc.gov/health-literacy/php/about/index.html) | Organizational health literacy: help people find, understand, and use information and services | A design principle, not proof of participant outcomes |
| [CDC: Plain Language Materials and Resources](https://www.cdc.gov/health-literacy/php/develop-materials/plain-language.html) | Audience-first organization, important information first, logical chunks, direct language | Plain language still requires source, context, and user review |
| [W3C: Web Content Accessibility Guidelines 2.2](https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/) | Accessibility target and testable success criteria, including keyboard, focus, labels, reflow, target size, help, and error prevention | Automated testing alone does not establish full conformance for every user or assistive technology |
| [NIST Privacy Framework](https://www.nist.gov/privacy-framework) | Privacy risk-management vocabulary and data-processing review | Voluntary risk framework; not a certification or healthcare-specific legal opinion |
| [HHS: Covered Entities and Business Associates](https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/covered-entities/index.html) | Defines the actual covered-entity/business-associate relationship that must be evaluated | A healthcare audience or public software product does not itself settle business-associate status |
| [HHS: Business Associate Contracts](https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/covered-entities/sample-business-associate-agreement-provisions/index.html) | Due-diligence reference if a future service would involve a business-associate relationship | No BAA or compliance claim is implied for the current public product |
| [HHS: Cloud Computing](https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/special-topics/health-information-technology/cloud-computing/index.html) | Reinforces that actual access to or maintenance of ePHI changes obligations | Avoiding requested ePHI is an architectural control, not a substitute for legal review |

## Market conclusion

The comparator set is materially stronger than CAF in enterprise infrastructure, human service, care delivery, integration, payment, account management, and validated commercial scale. CAF can credibly lead only in a narrower category that the current product can demonstrate:

- public, healthcare-specific financial education and decision preparation;
- working tools visible before procurement;
- transparent source, math, assumptions, uncertainty, and scope;
- no-account/no-participant-file starting architecture;
- buyer implementation and due-diligence planning generated without free text;
- explicit guidance to choose another vendor when the requirement falls outside this category.

No statement that CAF is categorically better than every competitor is supported by this review. Any comparative copy must state the dimension, evidence, review date, and limitation.
