# CAF Patient Guide Editorial Governance

## Risk tiers

1. General education: roles, communication, organization, and questions.
2. Coverage and workflow: observation, rehabilitation, home health, bills, and authorization.
3. Clinical safety education: blood thinners, COPD, heart failure, oxygen, and medication changes.
4. Withheld individualized instruction: dosing, missed-dose rules, treatment changes, oxygen settings, procedure timing, universal emergency thresholds, and personalized symptom decisions.

## Page controls

Health guides display author, credentials, dates, review scope, review status, authoritative sources, limits, related resources, and a correction route. RN authorship is not independent medical review. Review claims require documented review of the exact version.

## Sources

Prefer official agencies, controlling documents, National Library of Medicine resources, recognized guidelines, official product information, and original research. RN experience may frame questions but is not the sole evidence for clinical facts.

## Privacy and ads

Use fixed-choice navigation, not free-text health collection. Analytics may contain only sanitized fixed identifiers. Keep medication, oxygen, emergency, checklist, source, correction, and interactive patient surfaces ad-free by default.

## Corrections

Correct material errors promptly. Suspend a page when a safety-critical claim or source cannot be verified. Do not change dates merely to appear fresh.
