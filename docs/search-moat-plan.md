# Community Acquired Finance Search Moat Plan

Last updated: 2026-07-06

## Purpose

This document keeps SEO work aligned with the site mission: simple, trustworthy financial literacy for healthcare workers, patients, caregivers, and families. The goal is not to stuff keywords into pages. The goal is to build useful pages around real confusion points where larger government, insurer, hospital, or retirement-plan websites are accurate but hard to understand.

## Editorial rules

- Use exact-question titles only when the page directly answers that question.
- Answer the core question in the first 50-80 words.
- Keep paragraphs short and practical.
- Use examples, common mistakes, calculators, checklists, and source sections.
- Link every search-intent article to a relevant hub, tool, glossary entry, or related guide.
- Do not add insurer rankings, affiliate calls to action, lead forms, popups, or scare tactics.
- Do not imply individualized medical, insurance, tax, legal, or investment advice.

## Internal linking pattern

- `/medicare-care-costs` should surface the exact-question Medicare articles.
- Each Medicare exact-question article should link back to `/medicare-care-costs` or `/medicare-care-costs#cost-estimator`.
- Medicare rehab, long-term care, Medicaid, and discharge pages should reinforce each other.

## Search Console review loop

Review weekly:

- Impressions by query.
- Pages with impressions but low click-through rate.
- Queries ranking positions 8-30.
- Pages with no impressions after indexing.
- Any page title that Google appears to rewrite heavily.

Improve pages only when the change would help a real reader: clearer answer, better title, stronger internal links, better example, better source note, or a more relevant calculator link.
