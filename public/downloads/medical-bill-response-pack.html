Medical Bill Response Pack

A printable, privacy-minimized set of checklists and worksheets for identifying documents, comparing an EOB with a provider bill, organizing calls, checking financial assistance, and tracking deadlines.

Start here: An Explanation of Benefits (EOB) is generally not a bill. Identify the document, confirm claim processing, and compare the written documents before treating a first balance as final.

Privacy rule: Do not write Social Security numbers, full member IDs, full claim numbers, full account numbers, diagnoses, procedure details, or other sensitive health information in this pack. Use shortened references only when needed to distinguish documents.

Educational use only: This pack organizes questions and documents. It does not determine coverage, coding accuracy, medical necessity, legal liability, appeal success, collectibility, or what a person owes.

1. First 15-Minute Checklist

Use this sequence before making the first payment or calling several organizations at once.

Identify the document: EOB, Medicare Summary Notice, provider bill, denial, financial-assistance form, estimate, or collection notice.
Confirm the date of service and billing organization.
Check whether the payer processed the claim and whether it is pending, adjusted, or denied.
Locate the patient-responsibility amount on the payer explanation.
Compare that amount with the provider bill.
Separate facility charges from physician, laboratory, imaging, anesthesia, pathology, and ambulance charges.
Request an itemized bill when the balance is large, unclear, or unexpected.
Check network processing and current surprise-billing protections when relevant.
Check hospital financial assistance before agreeing to a long payment plan or using high-interest debt.
Record every department, date, reference, promise, and deadline.
Stop point: When the EOB, provider bill, and portal do not show the same patient responsibility, pause and ask the organization that owns the inconsistent number for a written explanation.

4. Itemized-Bill Request Script

Adapt this script to the communication method the billing organization provides. Keep a dated copy.

Suggested request: I am reviewing the balance for services on [service date]. Please provide a complete itemized bill showing each charge, adjustment, payment, and current patient-responsibility amount. Please also confirm whether all related insurance or program claims have been processed and whether the account can be held while I compare the itemized bill with the payer explanation.

Optional questions

Which billing organizations may send separate bills from this visit?
Does this balance reflect the most recent payer processing?
What does each adjustment or write-off represent?
Is any claim pending, corrected, resubmitted, or under review?
What is the written billing-dispute process?
Can collection activity pause while the account is reviewed?

8. Questions to Ask Before Paying

What document is this, and is it actually requesting payment?
Has the correct insurer or program processed every related claim?
Does the patient-responsibility amount match the payer explanation?
Are facility and professional charges separated correctly?
Is an itemized bill available?
Is any claim pending, corrected, resubmitted, denied, or appealed?
Does a network or surprise-billing issue need current official review?
Does the hospital have a financial-assistance policy that may apply?
Would paying now affect a pending assistance, dispute, or appeal process?
What written deadline applies, and who owns the next action?
